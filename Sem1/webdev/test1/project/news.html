Brief description of the Lady Blackbird system

01.08.2020 // by Wizzard_Rick

One of the many mini-game systems, Lady Blackbird stood out for me in that her rulebook takes only 16 pages.The book can be downloaded for free at .The system uses cubes d6. The mechanic is based on throwing a set of dice and getting the number of successes necessary for solving the problem (dice on which 4 or more have dropped out). Initially, the set consists of one cube and can be increased by:

- Skills that describe the character in a broad sense (such as "Smuggler", "Fighter", "Thief", or "Household Magic") - only one skill can be suitable for any action, giving +1 dice per roll.
- Tags are descriptors of skill subsections, such as "nimble" for a thief, or "dodge" for a pilot. If the action matches the skill, then you can select several tags that match the description of the action, and get +1 cube for each tag.
- Personal set - each character receives 7 cubes at the beginning of the session, and can spend them to increase the set of cubes of a specific action - but if successful, the cubes are not returned to the personal set.
- Keys - the main features of the character that are important for his development - such as “commander” for the captain of the ship or “bodyguard” for the guard.

Fiasco game review

03.08.2020 // by Guns_n_Droids

So Fiasco. What can a game with this name tell? Surely about losing. Who might want to play it, who might want to lose? Oh, many. To many. You will be surprised, but losing in Fiasco is an interesting experience. Some people even like to die in Fiasco. How so? - you ask, and I will answer you - you will soon find out. So, we roughly imagine what the Fiasco game is about . Now it should be explained how it differs from any other role-playing game.

In short, the fact that (at least in the usual sense of the word) Fiasco is not a role-playing game . In Fiasco, several players get together to create a story. There is no leader, no party, no movement towards a common goal. Even with a certain influence of the dice, it is the players who play the main role in shaping the plot.

Angels work, fight, intrigue and try to find some meaning in this, which becomes more difficult in the absence of God. And some of them believe that it would be high time to carry out the zeroing of the existing order - maybe the Creator will return after the Last Judgment?

Ted Williams "Empire of Grass": the standard of the fantasy epic

07.08.2020 // by Dmitry Zlotnitsky

Thirty years have passed since the Storm King's War. All this time, Austen Ard enjoyed peace under the wise rule of King Simon and Queen Miriamel. But the decades of peace have come to an end - the immortal norns once again invade human lands, seeking revenge for past defeats. Meanwhile, the kingdoms of people are on the verge of bloody feuds, betrayal ripened at the court of the High King, and the young and frivolous heir to the throne disappears during a diplomatic mission.

It has everything you would expect from a fantasy epic: cunning intrigues, bloody battles, exciting adventures, interesting character development. At the same time, despite the abundance of events, "The Empire of Grass" cannot be called either hasty or chaotic, which could have happened if the book was written by a less skilled author. Williams finds the perfect pace for the story: not in a hurry, but at the same time always keeps the reader in suspense.

No One Gets Out Alive: A Novel

24.07.2020 // by Irina Nechaeva

Stephanie rents a room that "looks like a potential suicide site after a long period of depression, loneliness and poverty", the main advantage of which is simply enchanting cheapness. But given the difficult financial situation in which the girl found herself, this is just a gift from heaven. True, the house where she settled had previously belonged to the Spiritualist Society. At night, voices and footsteps are heard in it, Stephanie will have to endure horrors, cold, darkness, become a victim of the homeowner, and then also deal with an ancient otherworldly entity...

"No One Gets Out Alive" doesn't waste time rocking - we learn about the horrors of 82 Edgehill Road in the very first chapter. Noise, voices, crying, creak of floorboards, a mattress unexpectedly sagging under an invisible body ... After one night in such a house, you need to run, but Stephanie has nowhere to run and nothing. And then it gets worse and worse. However, this story is not really about a haunted house. More precisely, not only about him. The ghosts of 82 are scary enough on their own, but the worst monsters are homeowner Drach McGwire and his cousin Fergal. These are not just scum of society, evil, uneducated and cruel, but real hardened criminals. If they don't like something, they easily turn to extortion and violence. They are described very realistically, and it is to them that we owe the most eerie scenes in the novel.
